what is Brain Infarct ?

A brain infarct, also known as a stroke, occurs when blood flow to a part of the brain is blocked or reduced. This lack of blood supply deprives brain cells of oxygen and nutrients, causing them to become damaged or die. Strokes can be of two main types: ischemic strokes, caused by a clot blocking a blood vessel, and hemorrhagic strokes, caused by a ruptured blood vessel. Symptoms can include weakness on one side of the body, difficulty speaking, vision problems, and sudden headaches. It's crucial to seek medical help promptly to minimize brain damage and improve recovery chances.
