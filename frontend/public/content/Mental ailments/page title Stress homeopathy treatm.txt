what is Stress?

Stress is that feeling we get when life throws a lot at us, like tight deadlines, traffic jams, or personal challenges. It's like our body's built-in alarm system, getting us ready to tackle tough situations—it's what we call the "fight or flight" response.

When we're stressed, our brain sends out hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. These hormones give us a burst of energy, make our heart race a bit, and help us focus better, which can be super helpful when we need to act fast.

But if stress sticks around too long, it can cause problems. It might show up as headaches, tense muscles, tummy troubles, or even mess with our mood and make us feel down.

So, how do we deal with stress? Well, it's all about finding ways to chill out. Things like taking deep breaths, trying out mindfulness or meditation, staying active with exercise, eating well, getting good sleep, and chatting with friends or a pro if things feel overwhelming—these are all ways to hit the "reset" button and build up our resilience to stress.
