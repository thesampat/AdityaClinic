what is Late Learning to talk

Certainly! Late Learning to Talk is when a child takes a bit longer than expected to start speaking and understanding language. It's like when your little one seems to be slower in picking up words compared to other kids their age. This delay can happen because every child grows and learns at their own speed. Reasons could range from needing more time to process sounds and words, or it might be due to hearing issues or other unique factors.

The good news is that with patience, support, and sometimes a little extra help, most children catch up just fine. Parents and caregivers play a huge role by encouraging communication, reading stories, and talking with their child. Sometimes, consulting with speech therapists or specialists can provide strategies and exercises to boost language skills. The key is understanding that every child is different, and they'll get there in their own time with a bit of love and encouragement along the way.
