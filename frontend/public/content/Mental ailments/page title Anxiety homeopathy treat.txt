what is Anxiety?

Anxiety is like your body's natural warning system that kicks in when you're feeling worried or stressed. Just think of it as a little alarm bell going off to get your attention. We all feel anxious at times, like before a big test or meeting new people—it's pretty normal.

But for some folks, anxiety can be a bit too loud. It's like that alarm just won't turn off, making everyday things feel like emergencies. You might notice racing thoughts, a fast heartbeat, sweaty hands, or a tight chest.

Remember, lots of people deal with anxiety, and there are ways to manage it. Talking to someone you trust, trying relaxation exercises, staying active, or even using certain medications can help calm that alarm down and bring back a sense of calm. It's all about finding what works best for you to feel more relaxed and in control.
