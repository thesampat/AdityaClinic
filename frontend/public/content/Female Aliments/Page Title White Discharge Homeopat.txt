What is White Discharge?

White discharge, also known as leukorrhea, is totally normal and happens to most women. It's this milky or clear liquid that comes out of the vagina. This stuff does a great job keeping the vaginal area clean and moist, which is super important for keeping everything down there healthy.

The amount and how thick this white stuff is can change during the month because of hormones. Sometimes, like around ovulation or right before periods, you might notice more or that it gets thicker.

Usually, this white discharge is all good, especially if it doesn't have a weird smell, itch, or cause irritation. But if it changes color, smell, or gets clumpy, it could be a sign of an infection or something else going on. That's when it's smart to talk to a doctor or nurse to check things out.

So basically, white discharge is just your body doing its thing to keep your lady parts healthy. Keeping an eye on any changes helps make sure everything stays in tip-top shape.
